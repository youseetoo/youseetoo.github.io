Extensive Checklist for Product Launch​

1. Title Page and Documentation Setup​

  • Product name, model number, and version clearly specified.
  • Manufacturer’s full name and address included.
  • Creation date of the documentation specified.
  • Ensure documentation is updated and saved in an easily accessible format (e.g., PDF).

2. Inhaltsverzeichnis (Table of Contents)​

  • Full table of contents created and matches the structure of the CE marking documentation.
  • Each section properly labeled with corresponding page numbers.

3. Product Description​

  • General product description completed, including the primary function and usage environment.
  • Technical specifications:
    • Complete hardware and software specifications listed (Raspberry Pi, ESP32, motors, camera, etc.).
    • Details about 3D-printed parts, optics, power supply, wiring, etc.
  • Intended use clearly defined, with potential usage scenarios and limitations outlined.
  • Ensure the description addresses the safety and environmental factors (e.g., IP rating, water exposure limits).

4. Declaration of Conformity​

  • A written Declaration of Conformity completed.
  • All relevant EU Directives identified (e.g., Machinery Directive, EMC Directive, Low Voltage Directive, RoHS).
  • Harmonized standards identified and listed (e.g., EN 60950 for electrical safety, EN 55032 for EMC).
  • The declaration signed and dated by the authorized person in your company.
  • Ensure the Declaration of Conformity is accessible and included in the product documentation package.

5. Risk Assessment and Analysis​

  • Full risk assessment conducted, including identification of all potential hazards.
  • A structured risk analysis method applied (e.g., FMEA or HAZOP).
  • Risk mitigation measures defined for each identified risk.
  • Residual risks (those that couldn't be fully eliminated) clearly documented and communicated.
  • Ensure all identified risks related to mechanical parts (e.g., motors) and electronic components (e.g., heat from the Raspberry Pi, electrical hazards) have been mitigated.
  • Check for water protection (e.g., ensure adequate sealing for IP-rated parts).
  • Risks from overheating, mechanical malfunction, and exposure to water documented.

6. Technical Drawings and Bill of Materials (BOM)​

  • Detailed technical drawings created for each component and assembly.
  • Exploded views of the product for easier assembly reference.
  • Complete Bill of Materials (BOM):
    • All parts (3D-printed, purchased, custom) listed with part numbers.
    • Specifications and supplier information included for each part.
    • Ensure availability of replacement parts and suppliers for mass production.
  • Document any critical tolerances or assembly instructions specific to key components (e.g., lens alignment, motor installations).

7. Test Reports and Certificates​

  • Electrical safety tests (e.g., insulation resistance, grounding, voltage withstand) conducted and documented.
  • EMC tests performed (radiated emissions, immunity, etc.).
  • Thermal tests on the Raspberry Pi and other components under full load documented.
  • Water and dust protection tests, if applicable (based on IP rating).
  • Certificates from third-party testing bodies obtained and included.
  • Ensure all test results confirm compliance with the applicable harmonized standards.

8. User Manual and Safety Instructions​

  • User manual created with detailed installation, operation, and maintenance instructions.
  • Clearly define startup procedures and first-time setup.
  • Operating instructions include:
    • Instructions for using the touchscreen and software (e.g., ImSwitch).
    • Safe handling of moving parts (motors) and optical components.
  • Safety instructions clearly explain:
    • Electrical safety (handling power supply, avoiding electrical shock).
    • Overheating risks, and warnings about touching hot surfaces.
    • Water damage risks and IP protection details.
    • Maintenance requirements (e.g., cleaning, part replacement).
  • Include clear labeling of hazard warnings in the manual (e.g., electrical shock, pinch points, etc.).
  • Ensure the language of the manual is clear and easy to understand for the intended audience.

9. Labels and Markings​

  • CE marking properly affixed to the product in a visible location.
  • Ensure that all necessary markings (e.g., WEEE, RoHS, safety certifications) are present on the product or packaging.
  • Packaging labels include:
    • Correct product information (model, power rating, etc.).
    • Warnings and regulatory marks where applicable.
  • IP Rating documented and displayed if the product has ingress protection (water/dustproof).

10. Additional Documents (if required)​

  • Software documentation created:
    • Code structure and architecture explained.
    • User access to source code or licenses (if applicable) provided.
  • Ensure documentation for any modifications or changes after certification is maintained.
  • Records of communication with regulatory bodies (if necessary) included.
  • Prepare a version history for the product, indicating any design or software updates post-certification.

11. Final Product Testing​

  • Conduct a full end-to-end product test (assembly, software, functionality).
  • Test the product under all expected operating conditions (temperature, humidity, power supply fluctuations).
  • Stress test key components (motors, Raspberry Pi, camera) under continuous operation.
  • Validate that all sensors and safety mechanisms (e.g., temperature monitoring, emergency stop) work correctly.
  • Ensure user interface (touchscreen, software) is responsive and user-friendly.

12. Production Readiness​

  • Mass production-ready: ensure all components and materials are available at scale.
  • Manufacturing process documented (assembly instructions, quality control checks).
  • Ensure supply chain stability for critical components (motors, optics, electronics).
  • Production tooling and fixtures tested and ready for use.

13. Legal and Commercial Preparations​

  • Ensure intellectual property (IP) is protected, and necessary patents, trademarks, or licenses are secured (if applicable).
  • Review contracts with suppliers and manufacturers to ensure legal and financial security.
  • Finalize pricing, distribution, and logistics plans for product launch.
  • Warranty information and terms of service included with the product documentation.
  • Finalize marketing and branding materials, ensuring that they comply with regulatory requirements.

14. Post-Launch Plans​

  • Establish customer support channels and documentation (e.g., FAQ, troubleshooting).
  • Set up a system for receiving and tracking customer feedback and complaints.
  • Prepare plans for future product updates, including software patches and hardware upgrades.
  • Ensure a process for handling product returns or failures is in place (e.g., product recall procedure).

Final Task Summary​

  • Document Review: Ensure all sections of the documentation are reviewed and approved.
  • Internal Review: Conduct a final internal review involving all relevant departments (engineering, quality control, legal).
  • External Audits: If required, schedule and complete any necessary external audits or inspections before product launch.
