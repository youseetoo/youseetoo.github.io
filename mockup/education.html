Teaching & Workshops

From high school physics to graduate-level biophotonics — openUC2 Discovery kits make optics tangible. Students don't just look through a microscope; they build one. Over 50 workshops delivered globally, from Jena to Addis Ababa.

[Photo: students at a workshop table, building microscopes from cubes]

Curriculum modules

Each module is a self-contained 2–4 hour teaching unit with learning objectives, materials, and assessment.

🔍 Geometric Optics

Lenses, mirrors, image formation. Students build a telescope, a projector, and a simple microscope. Kit: CoreBox

📱 Smartphone Microscopy

Turn a smartphone into a microscope. Image onion cells, water samples, printed circuits. Kit: CoreBox

⚡ Electronics & Automation

Introduction to microcontrollers, stepper motors, and automated image acquisition. Kit: CoreBox + Electronics Add-On

♾️ Infinity Optics

Why research microscopes use infinity-corrected systems. Build one and compare to the finite setup. Kit: + Infinity Add-On

🟢 Fluorescence

Excitation, emission, Stokes shift, dichroics. Image fluorescent samples on a system the students built themselves. Kit: + Fluorescence Add-On

📐 Light-Sheet

Selective plane illumination for 3D biology. Build a SPIM from cubes. Kit: + Light-Sheet Add-On
